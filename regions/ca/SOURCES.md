# Sources and discussion types used

This expansion prioritises Canadian self-description, regional/provincial usage and anti-stereotype notes rather than “eh/hockey/snow” caricature.

- Dictionary of Canadianisms on Historical Principles (DCHP-2): Canadian lexical items, regional labels and historical usage. https://www.dchp.ca/dchp2/
- The Canadian Style, Government of Canada / TERMIUM Plus: Canadian spelling, public-sector style and institutional wording. https://www.btb.termiumplus.gc.ca/tpv2guides/guides/tcdnstyl/index-eng.html?lang=eng
- Statistics Canada language and geography materials: province/territory, urban/suburban/rural and multilingual context; used as background for avoiding a single national voice. https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/
- Language Portal of Canada / Our Languages: Canadian English, bilingual terminology and usage discussions; used as background for Quebec and federal/bilingual cautions.
- Canadian Encyclopedia, “Canadian English”: overview of vocabulary, spelling and regional variation; used as background source.
- Local Canadian self-description patterns from municipal/provincial services, transit systems, school boards, CBC/local news, university, community radio and neighbourhood discussions; used for lived urban/suburban/regional/rural signals.
- Locale marker seed set added 2026-05-19: provincial utilities/liquor/insurance agencies, transit systems/cards, regional food terms, road/geography phrases, and northern logistics/institutions. Markers are evidence only; many appear in travel, policy, recipe, or interprovincial comparison contexts.
- Anti-trope review lens: avoid constant “eh”, hoser comedy, hockey-only identity, empty-North framing, and treating Canada as simply “nicer America”; choose province/city/community context first.

## Sports locality data

Added `data/sports_locality.csv` as a reviewable sports-cultural context layer for CA localities. Rows cover top local sports/codes, locally salient teams, a few current roster/player names, and notable historic players. Sources are primarily official league/team roster pages and established historical/team pages listed in the CSV `sources` column.

Caution: sports references are weak cultural evidence, not identity proof. Do not infer residence, class, ethnicity, religion, politics, or exact local allegiance from a team, code, or player mention. Current-player fields should be refreshed from official rosters before high-stakes or current-events use.

## Daily-life institutions and media ecology

Added `data/institutional_context.csv` and `data/media_reference_ecology.csv` for CA locality context. Institutional rows favour public-service, utility, health, education, transport, and admin systems that locals actually navigate. Media/reference rows map public broadcasters, local news, weather/emergency channels, and civic/event reference layers.

Caution: these rows are context-tree evidence, not identity proof. A person can mention a service, broadcaster, newspaper, or event because they visited, read about it, worked on it, or are helping someone else. Use several independent clues before trusting a locality inference, and avoid fake-local name-dropping.

## Generational cultural quote/reference bank

Added `data/cultural_quote_references.csv` for CA country-level cultural reference context. Rows are organised by generation and include films, TV, comedy, internet references, civic/sport references, short quote fragments, usage notes, avoid-when guidance, cautions, and source hints.

Caution: these references are weak cultural evidence, not identity or nationality proof. They are taste-, age-, class-, media-diet-, and community-shaped. Quote fragments are intentionally short reference handles; do not reproduce long copyrighted passages or force catchphrases into serious writing.
